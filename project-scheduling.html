Gantt Charts

Experiment No 3

Aim: Design Project Schedule using Gantt Chart

Objective:

To plan and schedule project activities in a systematic manner.

Theory:

Project scheduling is the process of planning, organizing, and controlling project activities so that the project is completed within the specified time and resources. One of the most commonly used tools for project scheduling is the Gantt Chart.

A Gantt Chart is a graphical representation of a project schedule that displays project activities along the vertical axis and time along the horizontal axis. Each activity is represented by a horizontal bar whose length indicates the duration of the task. The position of the bar shows the start and end time of each activity.

The Gantt chart was introduced by Henry L. Gantt and is widely used in project management because of its simplicity and clarity. It helps project managers and team members understand what tasks need to be completed, when they should start, and how long they will take.

Advantages of Using Gantt Chart

  • Provides a clear and simple visual representation of the project schedule.
  • Helps in effective planning and time management.
  • Enables easy tracking of project progress.
  • Assists in identifying task dependencies and critical activities.
  • Improves coordination and communication among project team members.

Limitations of Gantt Chart

  • Not suitable for very large and complex projects.
  • Does not clearly show complex interdependencies between tasks.
  • Requires frequent updates to remain accurate.
  • Changes in schedule may require redrawing the chart.
